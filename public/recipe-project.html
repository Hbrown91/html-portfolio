Cajun Corn Chowder

Serves: 6-8

Total Time: 45 minutes

If you’re craving something warm, creamy, and just a little bit spicy, Cajun Corn Chowder is about to become your new favorite food. It’s got that perfect mix of sweet corn, smoky sausage, and creamy goodness with just enough spice to keep things interesting without setting your mouth on fire. Basically...

It’s comfort food with personality.

Once you try it, it’s dangerously easy to keep going back for “just one more bowl.”

Ingredients

  • 8 cups frozen corn, thawed
  • 3 cups of low-sodium chicken broth
  • 8 oz andouille sausage, cut into 1/4" pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped fine
  • 1 red bell pepper, stemmed, seeded, and chopped fine
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 tsp Cajun seasoning
  • 1 lb red potatoes, cut into 1/2" pieces
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Process 4 cups corn and 2 cups broth in blender until smooth (about 10 seconds). Set aside.

  2. Combine sausage, onion, bell pepper, and oil in Dutch oven and cook over medium-high heat until vegetables are softened and lightly browned (5-7 minutes).

  3. Stir in garlic and Cajun seasoning and cook until fragrant (about 30 seconds).

  4. Stir in remaining 1 cup broth, scraping up any browned bits.

  5. Stir in pureed corn, potatoes, and cream. Simmer until potatoes are tender (about 20 minutes).

  6. Stir in remaining 4 cups corn and cook until heated through (about 1 minute).

  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve.
